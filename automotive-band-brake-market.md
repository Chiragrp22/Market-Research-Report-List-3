<p><p>Automotive band brakes come in two main types: simple brake band and differential brake band. Simple brake bands are used in traditional braking systems where both ends of the band are connected to a lever. On the other hand, differential brake bands have one end attached to a lever while the other end is fixed, allowing for more precise control over braking pressure. Both types are essential components in automotive braking systems, providing reliable and efficient stopping power for vehicles.</p></p>
