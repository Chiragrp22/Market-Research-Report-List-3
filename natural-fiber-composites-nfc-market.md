The construction and automotive sectors are the major consumers of NFC, as these materials are used in the production of interior parts, structural components, and panels. The growing focus on reducing carbon emissions and promoting green building practices is further propelling the market growth.</p><p>The NFC market is also witnessing technological advancements, leading to the development of innovative products. For instance, the use of nanotechnology to modify the natural fibers and improve their mechanical properties has gained traction in recent years. Additionally, the incorporation of natural fibers in biodegradable polymers is gaining popularity, as it offers better recyclability and sustainability.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a shift towards the use of natural fibers derived from sustainable sources, such as agricultural waste and recycled materials. This trend is driven by the growing concern for deforestation and the need to reduce dependence on non-renewable resources.</p><p>According to the forecast, the NFC market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.5% during the forecast period.
