<p><p>Human Acellular Vessels (HAVs) are bioengineered grafts used in vascular surgery for the replacement of damaged or diseased blood vessels. HAVs are derived from human donor tissue, chemically processed to remove all cellular components resulting in an acellular matrix that retains the architecture and mechanical properties of native blood vessels. These grafts are then repopulated with the patient's own cells upon implantation.</p><p>The HAV market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases and the growing demand for minimally invasive surgical procedures are the primary drivers of market growth. HAVs offer several advantages over traditional synthetic grafts, including reduced risk of infection, lower rates of graft rejection, and improved long-term outcomes. These benefits are driving the adoption of HAVs in vascular surgeries, thereby boosting the market growth.</p><p>In addition, technological advancements in tissue engineering and regenerative medicine are further propelling the market. Researchers are continuously working on improving the decellularization process, enhancing the biomechanical properties of HAVs, and increasing their bioactivity. This ongoing research and development activities are likely to result in the development of advanced HAVs, which will fuel market growth.</p><p>Moreover, the increasing geriatric population and the rising prevalence of diabetes and obesity are expected to contribute to market growth. These conditions often lead to vascular complications, creating a need for vascular grafts.
