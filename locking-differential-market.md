<p><p>Locking differentials are used in vehicles to provide better traction in off-road or slippery conditions. There are three main types of locking differentials: Automatic Locking Differential, which engages automatically when slip is detected; Selectable Locking Differential, which can be manually engaged by the driver; and Spool, a type of locking differential where both wheels are always locked together. Each type has its own advantages and disadvantages, appealing to different consumers based on their needs and preferences for off-road performance.</p></p>
