<p><p>Cancer mouse models are used in research and drug development for studying and understanding cancer. Humanized Immune System Mouse Models involve introducing human immune cells into mice to mimic the human immune response. Syngeneic Tumor Mouse Models use mouse cancer cells from the same strain of mice to study tumor growth and response to treatment. Immunodeficient Mouse Models lack a functional immune system and are useful for studying tumor development and testing therapies. NOG Mouse Models are immunodeficient mice with human immune system components. Spontaneous Tumor Mouse Models occur naturally in mice and mimic human cancer. Other types refer to different variations or combinations of these models.</p></p>
