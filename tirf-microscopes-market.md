<p><ul><li>Prism Method</li><li>Objective Lens Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>TIRF microscopes are commonly classified into two main types: the Prism Method and the Objective Lens Method. In the Prism Method, a specialized prism is used to direct the excitation light at a steep angle into the sample, allowing for highly selective illumination of the surface. On the other hand, the Objective Lens Method involves adjusting the angle of the excitation light through the objective lens, enabling precise control over the penetration depth into the sample. Both methods offer unique advantages and are preferred based on specific research requirements.</p></p>
